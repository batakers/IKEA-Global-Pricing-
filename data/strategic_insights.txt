IKEA Global Pricing Strategy - Strategic Insights

5 Strategic Business Insights:
1. United States is the most expensive market with average price $201.88, while Slovenia is the cheapest at $152.43.
2. Price positioning is most premium in United States (price index 1.14) and most value-oriented in Slovenia (price index 0.86).
3. Affordability pressure is highest in Germany (index 0.0034) and lowest in United States (index 0.0025).
4. United States shows the highest price dispersion (std 387.15), indicating mixed pricing tiers or assortment mix.
5. United States has the widest assortment breadth (177 sub-categories), while digital availability is weakest in Croatia (88.9% online).

3 Strategic Recommendations:
1. Introduce market-specific entry-price SKUs in Germany and similar high-pressure markets to improve affordability without eroding global brand value.
2. Review premium pricing justification in United States using competitor benchmarks and elasticity testing to protect volume growth.
3. Prioritize e-commerce enablement in Croatia and low-online peers to expand reach, especially for categories with strong global demand.
